End Game

  • Originality: The artwork and smooth movement of pieces in a turn based game
  • Technical Merit: The pieces are moved by velocity as opposed to a simple relocation, tilemaps are also used.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? - tile maps can be hard... What was the easiest or hardest part of making it? - the hardest thing was creating all of the tile artwork
  • Prototype Assets: Did you make your prototype assets from scratch? Did you borrow them? Cite your sources here.

  • Prototype Closest Other Game: the combat in any civilization game
    • The code for this example is adapted from the Phaser Basic Project Template
  • High Concept: Kill the other team
  • Theme: Inspiration from "cyborg that thinks its human". I tried to make the tileset look sci-fi, though it looks more like glass panes.
  • Mandated Variety: Mouse, Board Games, Turn Based Strategy
  • Prototype Goal: Board game
  • Player Experience Goals: quick tactical thinking to beat opponents
  • Gameplay: There are two teams. One yellow, and one green. Move a unit by clicking the middle mouse button on it to select it, and then left click on an empty square to move it. To shoot the other team, select one of your units and left click on an enemy unit. Last team standing wins!
  • Strategies: Cheating by taking multiple shots in one turn.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: The idea is that there are only these floating balls left after some great war. They're still fighting each other, and they don't know or remember why. They just do what their programming tells them.
  • Target Audience: The turn-based-strategy audience
  • Play Time: Depends on the skill level difference in the players
